## What it is

**perma.ws** üßä represents the future of decentralized web archiving: provable, verifiable, and permanent. It's a decentralized archiving service that creates cryptographically-attested snapshots of web pages that can never be altered, deleted, or lost.

Every archive on perma.ws includes both a visual screenshot and a high-fidelity interactive archive (WACZ format) that preserves the full experience of the original webpage. But unlike traditional archiving services, every snapshot comes with a cryptographic attestation proving its authenticity.

With perma.ws, you can finally trust that what you're seeing is exactly what existed at that moment in time. The archiving process is provably done in a Nautilus Trusted Execution Environment (TEE), and archives are stored permanently on Walrus decentralized storage.

This creates an immutable record of the web that serves researchers, journalists, legal professionals, and anyone who needs to preserve and prove what a webpage looked like at a specific point in time.

The result? A web where nothing is permanent, nothing is provable, and trust is questionable.

## Our Solution

### üîí **Cryptographic Attestation**
Every archive is captured inside a **Nautilus Trusted Execution Environment (TEE)** running on AWS Nitro Enclaves. The TEE cryptographically signs each archive with an **Ed25519 signature**, creating an attestation that proves:
- The exact URL that was archived
- The precise timestamp of capture
- The SHA-256 hash of the screenshot
- The Walrus blob ID of the WACZ archive
- That the capture was performed by genuine TEE hardware, not manipulated software

You can verify the attestation independently. No trust required.

### üåä **Permanent Decentralized Storage**
All archives are stored on **Walrus decentralized storage**, ensuring they can never be:
- Deleted by a platform or government
- Lost in a server crash or company shutdown
- Censored by a corporation
- Held hostage behind paywalls or access restrictions

Once stored on Walrus, your archives exist forever. The network guarantees availability even if individual nodes fail.

### üì∏ **High-Fidelity Capture**
perma.ws doesn't just save HTML. Every archive includes:
- **Screenshot**: A pixel-perfect visual capture via ScreenshotOne
- **WACZ Archive**: A complete interactive archive generated by Scooper that preserves JavaScript, CSS, images, fonts, and dynamic content
- **Full browsing experience**: Navigate the archived page exactly as it appeared, with working links and interactions

### üîç **Independent Verification**
Anyone can verify an archive's authenticity:
1. Download the attestation JSON
2. Verify the Ed25519 signature against the TEE's public key
3. Confirm the screenshot hash matches the stored image
4. Check the Walrus blob ID matches the WACZ archive
5. Validate the timestamp and URL

The math doesn't lie. If the signature is valid, the archive is genuine.

## How It Works

1. **Submit a URL** ‚Äî Enter any web address you want to archive
2. **TEE Capture** ‚Äî The Nautilus server running in a Trusted Execution Environment fetches the page, generates a screenshot, and creates a WACZ archive
3. **Cryptographic Signing** ‚Äî The TEE signs an attestation containing the URL, timestamp, screenshot hash, and Walrus blob ID
4. **Decentralized Storage** ‚Äî The screenshot and WACZ archive are uploaded to Walrus for permanent storage
5. **Verification** ‚Äî Anyone can download the attestation and independently verify its authenticity using standard cryptographic tools
